&lt;p&gt;Over years, as Indian handicrafts kept losing its charm and demand in the market, artisans took up alternative means to support their families. An opportunity of not only pushing the application of our own technology and design education to its limits but also being able to make a difference on a large scale. When a craft dies, not only do we lose the art objects but we also lose generations of faith and wisdom that went into the object.&amp;nbsp; Today when we remit payments to an artisan, earlier struggling for recognition, the warmth we get in return is a feeling we never imagined the gravity of at the onset,&amp;rdquo; says Dipti, one of the team members of this research&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
